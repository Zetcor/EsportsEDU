Fortnite Champion Series (FNCS) Global Championship: The Peak of
Competitive Fortnite

The FNCS (Fortnite Champion Series) was introduced by Epic Games in 2019 as Fortnite’s official ongoing competitive league. While FNCS initially consisted of online seasonal competitions, the FNCS Global Championship was introduced as the cumulative international finale, bringing together the best duos from around the world.

The Global Championship acts as the pinnacle of the FNCS ecosystem, following a year of rigorous regional competitions and point-based qualification systems. Though the first in-person Fortnite World Cup in 2019 was a landmark event, the Global Championship now functions as the spiritual successor, marking the peak of Fortnite’s esports calendar. The event’s reintroduction as a LAN event in 2023 signaled Epic’s renewed commitment to high-tier, live competitive Fortnite.

Organizer/Governing Body

Epic Games, the developer and publisher of Fortnite, is the sole organizer and governing body of FNCS and all associated competitive circuits.

Games Featured in the Tournament

Fortnite Battle Royale — a third-person, 100-player battle royale game incorporating building mechanics, mobility, and frequent gameplay changes through seasonal updates.

Tournament Format

The FNCS Global Championship brings together 75 top duos (150 players total), qualifying through regional Fortnite Champion Series (FNCS) events, including online qualifiers, Major tournaments, and Last Chance Qualifiers. These duos represent regions like North America, Europe, Brazil, Asia, the Middle East, Oceania, and Latin America, ensuring diverse international competition. The tournament begins with a Group Stage where the 75 duos are split into three groups of 25, each competing in six matches for points based on placement and eliminations.

The top duo from each group advances directly to the Grand Finals, while the remaining duos enter the Surge Stage for a second chance to qualify. In this high-stakes round, teams compete in 5-6 matches, with the top performers filling the remaining Grand Finals spots. The 50 duos in the Grand Finals play six final matches, with points from eliminations and placements determining the champion, rewarding strategic balance between survival and aggressive play.

Prize Pool

The FNCS Global Championship 2023 boasted a $4,000,000 USD prize pool, making it one of the largest single-event rewards in battle royale esports. Fully funded by Epic Games, the prize pool highlights the company's strong commitment to Fortnite's competitive scene. The winning duo earned $1,000,000 USD, split evenly between both players, while second and third place took home $650,000 and $400,000 respectively. Substantial payouts extended to all Grand Finals participants, with top 10 duos receiving six-figure prizes and even Surge Stage participants earning a share for qualifying. Epic Games also provided travel stipends and logistical support to ensure fairness and accessibility, reinforcing a competitive ecosystem that values both performance and inclusivity.

Live Streams and Coverage

Epic Games provides comprehensive coverage of the event, streaming on platforms such as Twitch, YouTube Gaming, Fortnite’s official website, TikTok, and X (formerly Twitter). Streams are available in multiple languages, including English, Spanish, Portuguese, French, German, Japanese, Arabic, and Korean. Fans can enjoy real-time map tracking, live elimination feeds, player face cams, and top team POVs during the broadcast, with analysts providing in-depth commentary.

Venue and Production

The production of the FNCS Global Championship showcases the best in esports broadcasting, with vibrant stage designs, extensive use of LED displays, and thrilling lighting effects. The event’s broadcasts are rich with player profiles, team rivalries, and the high-stakes action of the tournament. This attention to production detail ensures a captivating viewing experience for both hardcore Fortnite fans and casual viewers, making it a standout esports event worldwide.

Global Stage

The FNCS Global Championship is typically held in iconic esports venues such as New York, USA, and London, UK, chosen for their cutting-edge facilities and central location in major gaming markets. These venues are selected to provide both a top-tier competitive environment for the players and an immersive experience for the fans. With a global online following, the FNCS Global Championship draws audiences from all over the world, creating an electrifying atmosphere at each venue.
