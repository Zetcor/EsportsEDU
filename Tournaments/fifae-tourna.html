FIFAe World Cup: The Pinnacle of Competitive Virtual Football

The tournament was inaugurated in 2004 under the name FIFA Interactive World Cup (FIWC), a collaboration between FIFA, EA Sports, and PlayStation. It has since evolved into the FIFAe World Cup, now a central component of FIFA’s broader FIFAe global competitive gaming ecosystem. The competition has mirrored the growth of esports and football gaming, transitioning from casual online qualifiers to a fully professional, international LAN event.

Organizer/Governing Body

The Fédération Internationale de Football Association (FIFA) is the governing body, with support from EA Sports (formerly for game development) and FIFAe, the official esports subdivision of FIFA

Games Featured in the Tournament

EA SPORTS FC (formerly FIFA series by EA Sports), focusing on the 1v1 Ultimate Team mode

Tournament Format

The FIFAe World Cup begins with global online qualifiers, split into national and club-based pathways. Players earn points through ranked play, licensed qualifiers, and regional playoffs to secure a place among the Top 24 who advance to the LAN Grand Finals. This open structure allows top talent from various regions to converge on the global stage.

At the Grand Finals, the 24 players are divided into four groups of six, playing round-robin matches within their groups. The top four from each group—16 players in total—advance to the knockout stage, which features two-legged matchups with aggregate scoring. All matches, including the two-legged Grand Final, are played on PlayStation 5, ensuring equal conditions and a high level of competitive integrity.

Prize Pool

The FIFAe World Cup features a guaranteed $500,000 USD total prize pool, funded by FIFA in collaboration with global sponsors including Adidas, Visa, and Coca-Cola. The prize distribution follows a tiered structure, with the 1st place winner receiving $250,000 USD and the runner-up earning $100,000 USD. Players finishing in 3rd and 4th place each receive $25,000 USD, while those placing between 5th and 8th are awarded $10,000 USD each. Competitors ranking from 9th to 16th take home $5,000 USD apiece. This structured payout ensures meaningful rewards for all players reaching the knockout stage, underscoring the event's prestige and FIFA’s commitment to advancing professional virtual football.

Live Streams and Coverage

The FIFAe World Cup is broadcasted globally, with live coverage available on various platforms. The tournament is streamed in multiple languages, catering to a diverse audience. Major platforms include Twitch, YouTube Gaming, and Facebook Gaming, with localized streams for specific regions. The event also features extensive social media coverage, including highlights, interviews, and behind-the-scenes content, allowing fans to engage with the tournament in real-time.

Venue and Production

The production of the FIFAe World Cup is renowned for its exceptional quality, featuring professional stage designs, cutting-edge technology, and immersive lighting. The broadcasts focus on both the high-level gameplay and the personal journeys of the players, with captivating stories that add depth to the competition. These production elements combine to provide an entertainment experience that resonates with a wide audience, from casual fans to the dedicated esports community.

Global Stage

The FIFAe World Cup takes place in major cities with excellent sports infrastructure, such as London, UK, and Madrid, Spain, attracting both local and international audiences. These venues are selected to offer high-quality settings for global esports competitions, bringing together players and fans from around the world. The FIFAe World Cup’s location and environment create an unforgettable experience that highlights the growing popularity of esports on the global stage.
