The International

M-Series World Championship: The Summit of Mobile MOBA Esports

The M-Series World Championship began with M1 in 2019 as the premier global event for Mobile Legends: Bang Bang (MLBB), a mobile Multiplayer Online Battle Arena (MOBA) developed by Moonton Games, a subsidiary of ByteDance. The series was created to bring together the best MLBB teams from across the globe in an annual competition to crown the world champion.

Since its inaugural edition in Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia, the M-Series has grown substantially in scale, viewership, and global participation. Each edition of the tournament introduces broader regional qualifiers, improved production standards, and expanded prize pools. By M5 in 2023, the series featured over 20 teams from six continents and hit viewership peaks exceeding 5 million concurrent viewers — making it one of the largest mobile esports events worldwide.

Organizer/Governing Body

Moonton Games, through its esports division MLBB Esports, is the sole organizer and governing body of the M-Series, in partnership with regional tournament operators.

Games Featured in the Tournament

Mobile Legends: Bang Bang — a fast-paced, 5v5 multiplayer online battle arena (MOBA) game designed for mobile platforms.

Tournament Format

The M-Series World Championship hosts 16 to 22 elite Mobile Legends: Bang Bang (MLBB) teams, which qualify through regional leagues like the Mobile Legends: Professional League (MPL), regional qualifiers, and wild card spots. The tournament promotes global representation, featuring teams from regions including Southeast Asia, Brazil, North America, MENA, Latin America, EECA, and South Asia. The event starts with a Wild Card Stage, giving emerging teams a chance to secure a place in the Main Stage.

The Main Group Stage divides the teams into four groups, each containing four to six teams. They compete in a round-robin format with Best-of-3 matches, where the top two teams from each group advance to the Upper Bracket of the Playoffs. The Playoffs follow a double-elimination format, with Upper Bracket teams having a second chance, and most matches being Best-of-5. The tournament concludes with a Best-of-7 Grand Finals, held offline at a LAN venue for optimal gameplay conditions and a live audience experience.

Prize Pool

As of the M5 World Championship in 2023, the prize pool reached $900,000 USD, with future editions expected to surpass $1 million. Funded by Moonton Games, along with in-game purchases and sponsorships, this growing pool reflects the tournament’s rising stature in mobile esports. The championship team receives $300,000, while second, third, and fourth places earn $120,000, $80,000, and $55,000 respectively, with the remaining prize money distributed among other Playoff-qualified teams. Beyond team earnings, individual awards are also presented, including MVP honors and special recognitions for top kills, assists, and impactful plays. Winners may also receive exclusive in-game cosmetics, adding prestige and enhancing player visibility in the broader community.

Live Streams and Coverage

The M-Series World Championship is broadcast on platforms like YouTube Gaming, Facebook Gaming, TikTok Live, Twitch, and the MPL/MLBB app. The event features multilingual streams in English, Filipino, Bahasa Indonesia, Spanish, Portuguese, Arabic, Russian, Burmese, Khmer, Vietnamese, and Bengali, ensuring global accessibility. Professional casters, analysts, and content creators provide detailed coverage, including real-time updates, team highlights, and player insights.

Venue and Production

The tournament’s production is known for its vibrant stage setups and high-quality visual effects. The broadcast integrates the latest technology, with dynamic lighting and innovative camera work, creating an exciting and engaging viewing experience. The addition of team and player narratives elevates the spectacle, offering a compelling storyline that connects the audience to the action both on and off the screen.

Global Stage

The M-Series World Championship has been held in major esports hubs, with past editions hosted in cities like Jakarta, Indonesia, Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia, and Philippines. These cities are selected for their active gaming communities and state-of-the-art event venues, ensuring the tournament’s success. The diverse locations enhance the championship’s international appeal and draw fans from across the globe, giving each edition its own unique cultural atmosphere.
