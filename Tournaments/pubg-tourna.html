PUBG Global Championship: The Apex of Competitive Battle Royale

The PUBG Global Championship was officially introduced in 2019 as the ultimate culminating event in the competitive calendar for PUBG: Battlegrounds, developed by PUBG Studios under KRAFTON Inc. It succeeded the previous PUBG Global Invitational (PGI) series, refining the competitive structure into a more coherent, global system. Since its inception, PGC has brought together the world’s best teams from various regional leagues for a grand offline finale.

Organizer/Governing Body

KRAFTON Inc. and PUBG Esports serve as the main organizers of PGC. Regional qualifiers and leagues are coordinated by third-party partners under the oversight of PUBG Esports.

Games Featured in the Tournament

Only one game is featured—PUBG: Battlegrounds, a tactical, third- and first-person battle royale shooter known for its realistic mechanics, survival-based gameplay, and strategic depth.

Tournament Format

The PUBG Global Championship (PGC) features 32 top-tier teams drawn from four major regions: Asia, Asia-Pacific, Europe, and the Americas. Teams qualify through strong performances in regional competitions like the PUBG Continental Series (PCS) and select global partner invitations. The tournament spans multiple phases, beginning with a Group Stage where two groups of 16 teams (Groups A and B) compete across three days with reshuffled matchups to ensure balanced play.

The top eight teams from each group advance to the Winners Bracket, where the best eight secure Grand Finals spots, while the bottom eight head to the Last Chance Qualifier (LCQ). Meanwhile, earlier-eliminated teams battle in the Losers Bracket for a second shot at the finals. The Grand Finals stage includes 16 teams competing over 18 to 20 matches, with points awarded for placement and kills—ultimately crowning the team with the highest score as the PUBG World Champion.

Prize Pool

The prize pool for the PUBG Global Championship (PGC) exceeds $2 million USD, with funding sourced from KRAFTON, third-party sponsors, and in-game item sales, including commemorative PGC skins. In 2023, the total prize pool surpassed $2.5 million USD, with over $600,000 USD awarded to the 1st place team and approximately $250,000 USD given to the runner-up. Teams placing between 3rd and 5th received between $150,000 and $100,000 USD. The remaining prize money was distributed among the top 16 teams, alongside additional bonus awards for Most Valuable Player (MVP), most kills, and best play. Moreover, PGC incorporates a revenue-sharing system that enables teams to earn a portion of in-game sales, creating a more sustainable esports ecosystem while incentivizing fan engagement.

The PUBG Global Championship offers extensive global coverage, ensuring fans around the world can follow the action live. Venue and Production

PGC’s production values are exceptional, with large-scale stage setups, sophisticated lighting systems, and incredible visual displays that enhance the competitive atmosphere. The use of cinematic broadcasts, along with in-depth team and player stories, makes for a captivating experience, allowing fans to connect with the tournament on a deeper level, whether they are seasoned players or new viewers.

Global Stage

The PUBG Global Championship is held in cities with robust esports infrastructure and accessibility, such as Incheon, South Korea, and Los Angeles, USA. Each venue is carefully selected to accommodate the scale of the tournament, ensuring a competitive environment and an engaging live experience. The strategic choice of host cities contributes to the event’s global presence and prestige, attracting a worldwide audience both on-site and online.
