Battle Royale

What is Battle Royale

Battle Royale is an online multiplayer video game genre that blends last-man-standing gameplay with elements of exploration, scavenging, and survival on a large, shrinking map. Players typically start with minimal equipment and must search the environment for weapons, armor, and other resources to eliminate opponents while staying within a constantly reducing safe zone. The last player or team remaining wins the match. The shrinking play area forces players into more frequent encounters, ensuring the game remains fast-paced and exciting. Many Battle Royale games also feature unique mechanics, such as crafting, respawning, or using vehicles, to add variety and strategic depth. As the genre continues to evolve, developers experiment with new features, ensuring that each game brings something fresh to the table for both casual and competitive players.

The history of Battle Royale

1999 - 2010

Origins

The Battle Royale genre finds its origins in the 1999 Japanese novel Battle Royale by Koushun Takami, where students are forced to fight to the death on an isolated island, laying the conceptual foundation for the genre. This concept is brought to life in the 2000 film adaptation of the novel, which helps popularize the idea of survival arenas. During the early 2010s, various multiplayer games include "last man standing" modes, but they remain secondary to the main gameplay. These modes, while interesting, are small-scale and lack the expansive nature that would later define the genre. Despite this, the genre starts to grow, with more developers and players gravitating towards the idea of survival-based gameplay.

2013 - 2016

The Rise of Battle Royale

In 2013, Brendan "PlayerUnknown" Greene develops the Battle Royale mod for the military simulation game ARMA 2, which significantly expands player count and map size while adding mechanics like scavenging and a shrinking play area. The mod's success lays the foundation for the modern Battle Royale genre, introducing key elements that would become genre standards. Greene continues to refine the concept with a similar mod for ARMA 3 and also works as a consultant on H1Z1: King of the Kill, a game featuring large-scale, last-man-standing combat. H1Z1 further popularizes the genre, showcasing the potential of large player counts and survival elements in a more accessible form. These early steps build the hype and anticipation for a fully-realized Battle Royale game.

2017 - 2018

The Golden Age & Competitive Explosion

In March 2017, PlayerUnknownâ€™s Battlegrounds (PUBG) launches in early access on Steam, with its realistic military simulation approach and large player counts quickly attracting millions of players worldwide. PUBG's success helps popularize the Battle Royale genre, establishing it as a standalone gaming category. In September 2017, Fortnite Battle Royale is released as a free-to-play mode within Fortnite, offering unique building mechanics and a cartoonish art style that set it apart from other games in the genre. The accessibility and free-to-play model of Fortnite allow it to quickly gain widespread success, becoming a major competitor to PUBG. The popularity of both games establishes Battle Royale as one of the dominant genres in gaming.

2018 - Present

The Evolution of Battle Royale

Following the rise of PUBG and Fortnite, the Battle Royale genre continues to explode, with a variety of new titles entering the market across PC, consoles, and mobile platforms. In 2019, Apex Legends introduces hero characters with unique abilities, adding a tactical layer and offering a fresh twist to the traditional Battle Royale formula. Call of Duty: Warzone (2020) integrates with the popular Call of Duty franchise, introducing massive player counts and a "Gulag" respawn mechanic, further innovating the genre. The mobile gaming scene also sees rapid growth with the release of PUBG MOBILE (2018) and Fortnite Mobile (2018), bringing Battle Royale to a vast, new audience. Meanwhile, Fall Guys: Ultimate Knockout (2020) puts a family-friendly, platforming spin on the last-player-standing concept, adding more variety and broadening the appeal of Battle Royale games.

Current State of Battle Royale

The Battle Royale genre is still very much alive and kicking, although the initial explosive growth has somewhat leveled off. While some early giants have seen fluctuations in player base, the core concepts remain popular, and new titles continue to emerge, often with unique twists on the formula. The genre has also expanded beyond traditional shooting games, incorporating melee combat, magic, and other mechanics to offer a broader range of gameplay experiences. The introduction of innovative features, such as hero abilities and respawn mechanics, has kept the genre fresh and engaging for both new and returning players. Mobile platforms have played a significant role in keeping the genre accessible to a global audience, ensuring that Battle Royale remains relevant. Additionally, esports competitions continue to thrive, fostering a competitive scene that draws players from all over the world.

Regional Popularity

North America

Battle Royale games have a strong following in North America, with Fortnite being particularly popular due to its free-to-play model and unique mechanics. PUBG also has a significant player base, though Fortnite dominates the region. The competitive esports scene, especially for Fortnite, draws large audiences and professional players from across the continent. Players in North America often enjoy the cross-platform functionality of these games, allowing for widespread accessibility. Regional tournaments and events contribute to the genre's growing influence in the area.

Europe

Europe has a diverse and dedicated player base for Battle Royale games, with PUBG and Fortnite being the most notable titles. PUBG appeals to players seeking a more realistic military simulation, while Fortnite attracts those enjoying a more casual, cartoonish style and building mechanics. The popularity of Apex Legends has also grown in certain European countries, adding another dimension to the genre. Esports tournaments featuring Battle Royale games regularly draw large crowds in Europe, particularly for Fortnite. Asia

Asia In Asia, Battle Royale games like PUBG MOBILE and Free Fire have immense popularity, particularly in South Asia and Southeast Asia. PUBG MOBILE has a massive player base in countries like India and Indonesia, where mobile gaming is especially prevalent. Fortnite also has a dedicated following, but its presence is often overshadowed by mobile titles. The regionâ€™s gaming community is highly competitive, with mobile esports tournaments regularly held across multiple countries. Cultural preferences for mobile gaming contribute significantly to the widespread success of mobile Battle Royale titles in Asia.

Latin America

In Latin America, Battle Royale games are incredibly popular, with Fortnite and PUBG MOBILE leading the way. Fortnite has a strong presence, particularly among younger players, thanks to its free-to-play nature and social gameplay features. PUBG MOBILE also enjoys widespread popularity due to the accessibility of mobile gaming in the region. Latin American players are known for their passionate involvement in the genre, with a growing interest in competitive esports. The accessibility of mobile games allows a large portion of the population to participate in the Battle Royale craze, despite varied economic conditions across the region.
