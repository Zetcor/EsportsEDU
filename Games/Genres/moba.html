MOBA (Multiplayer Online Battle Arena)

General Information Popular Games

What is MOBA Esports

MOBA games revolve around two teams battling to destroy each other’s base on a fixed map with three primary lanes and a jungle area. Each player controls a single hero or champion with unique abilities and roles, such as tank, support, or damage dealer. The core gameplay mixes real-time combat, leveling, and strategic team fights.

Success in a MOBA depends heavily on teamwork, map control, and timing. Players must coordinate attacks, control key objectives, and respond to shifting momentum in fast-paced matches. League of Legends and Dota 2 are prime examples, with deep competitive scenes and massive esports tournaments.

The history of Multiplayer Online Battle Arena (MOBA)

1990s

Origins

The origins of MOBA trace back to StarCraft and Warcraft III custom maps. Notably, Aeon of Strife (StarCraft) and later Defense of the Ancients (DotA) (Warcraft III) introduced the core concept: teams of players controlling unique heroes battling in symmetrical arenas with AI-controlled units.

2000s

DOTA's Rise and Genre Formation

The 2000s saw DotA explode in popularity through Warcraft III's modding community, effectively creating the MOBA genre. Despite being unofficial, DotA’s deep gameplay, hero diversity, and competitive nature attracted a global following and laid the groundwork for standalone MOBAs.

2010s

MOBA Becomes Mainstream Esport

The launch of League of Legends (2009) and Dota 2 (2013) established MOBA as one of the most dominant genres in esports. Massive tournaments like The International and Worlds offered multi-million-dollar prize pools, elevating MOBAs to the top of global competitive gaming.

2020s

Diversification and Mobile Dominance

While core MOBAs remain popular, the genre has expanded into mobile gaming with titles like Mobile Legends: Bang Bang and Wild Rift, especially in Southeast Asia and Latin America. Cross-platform accessibility and shorter match formats have brought the genre to a broader, global audience.

Current State of the Multiplayer Online Battle Arena (MOBA)

Despite more than a decade on the market, the genre of MOBA, where League of Legends (LoL) and Dota 2 are still among the biggest MOBA games worldwide, have consistently rank among the most-watched games on streaming platforms. Additionally, both LoL and Dota 2 are inextricably linked with the growth of eSports and the two legacy titles still have some of the most attractive eSports tournament prize pools across all competitive titles - in 2024, the cumulative Dota 2 eSports prize pool was worth almost 24 million U.S. dollars.

Regional Popularity

China

MOBA games dominate China’s mobile gaming scene, with Honor of Kings leading as one of the most played titles in the world. Its integration with Tencent’s ecosystem and strong esports infrastructure has solidified its cultural presence, especially among younger audiences.

South Korea

In South Korea, MOBAs are a central part of the competitive gaming landscape. League of Legends remains the most popular title, with massive viewership, institutional esports support, and deep strategic play, making it a staple in both internet cafés and professional arenas.

Europe

Europe has a robust MOBA fanbase, particularly around League of Legends. The region features top-tier talent through the LEC (League of Legends EMEA Championship), and countries like Germany, France, and Poland boast thriving competitive and amateur ecosystems.

North America

In North America, MOBAs like League of Legends and Dota 2 have strong communities, especially among PC gamers. The region is home to the LCS and hosts international tournaments, but overall player engagement is more casual compared to Asia’s high-intensity competitive scene.
