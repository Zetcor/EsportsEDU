MOBA (Multiplayer Online Battle Arena)

What is Multiplayer Online Battle Arena (MOBA)

A MOBA, short for Multiplayer Online Battle Arena, is a real-time strategy video game in which two teams of players, usually 5 players per team, compete against each other on a predetermined battlefield. Each player controls a single character with a set of unique abilities that improve as the game progresses. Players can also upgrade their characters by buying items using gold gained from completing objectives or killing enemy characters. Generally, there are some towers that will shoot your creatures on the way, and maybe a few other nuances, but that’s the gist of it. Kill the base, win the game. Simple enough.

The history of Multiplayer Online Battle Arena (MOBA)

Pre 2000s

Origins

The origin of the first MOBA game is often debated by gamers. Many claim that the 1989 Sega game Herzong Zwei is the first MOBA game ever created while others argue the “Precinct Assault” mode, featured in the 1998 game Future Cop: LAPD, is the first real MOBA.

Regardless of who came first, most gamers will agree that Aeon of Strife, a custom StarCraft map, is the game that best started the genre’s movement in the pre 2000’s. In 1998, Blizzard Entertainment released StarCraft, one of their best-selling real-time strategy (RTS) PC games. The game included a suite of game editing tools called StarEdit that allowed users to design and create custom maps with custom rules and gameplay. StarCraft modder, Aeon64, created a custom map named Aeon of Strife that became immensely popular with the StarCraft community. Many of the key features found in Aeon of Strife eventually became the base structure for all future MOBAs.

2008 – present

Rise of the MOBA Genre

By 2008, the popularity of Dota had attracted commercial attention. Since the format was tied to the Warcraft property, developers began to work on their own "DOTA-style" video games. In late 2009, Riot Games' debut title League of Legends was released. It was initially designed by Steve Feak, one of the original creators of Dota: Allstars, who went on to apply many of the mechanics and lessons he learned from the mod.

Current State of the Multiplayer Online Battle Arena (MOBA)

Despite more than a decade on the market, the genre of MOBA, where League of Legends (LoL) and Dota 2 are still among the biggest MOBA games worldwide, have consistently rank among the most-watched games on streaming platforms. Additionally, both LoL and Dota 2 are inextricably linked with the growth of eSports and the two legacy titles still have some of the most attractive eSports tournament prize pools across all competitive titles - in 2024, the cumulative Dota 2 eSports prize pool was worth almost 24 million U.S. dollars.

Regional Popularity

Asia-Pacific

Asia-Pacific is the largest and most influential region for MOBAs. In Southeast Asia, mobile MOBAs such as Mobile Legends and Honor of Kings dominate the market. In China, MOBAs continue to enjoy massive popularity across both mobile and PC platforms. South Korea has a strong presence of PC MOBAs, with League of Legends being especially prominent. Meanwhile, in Japan, titles like Wild Rift and Pokémon Unite have gained significant traction among players.

North America

North America has established itself as a major market for MOBAs, particularly in the PC gaming sector. In the United States and Canada, popular titles such as League of Legends, Smite, and Heroes of the Storm have maintained steady player communities over the years. While PC gaming remains dominant, the mobile MOBA market is also experiencing rapid growth, driven by the increasing popularity of games like Mobile Legends: Bang Bang and Wild Rift.

Europe

In Europe, PC MOBAs are quite popular, with a strong and active player base across the region. Countries such as Germany, France, and the United Kingdom have thriving gaming cultures, where titles like Dota 2 and League of Legends enjoy a large and dedicated following. Esports tournaments featuring these games regularly draw large audiences both online and in live venues.

Latin America

In Latin America, MOBAs have become increasingly popular, with a particularly strong presence in countries such as Brazil, Argentina, and Mexico. League of Legends dominates the PC scene, while mobile MOBAs like Mobile Legends: Bang Bang are gaining traction due to their accessibility. The region has a vibrant esports community, with local tournaments and international events drawing significant attention. Brazilian teams, in particular, have made a name for themselves in international League of Legends competitions, contributing to the growth of competitive gaming in LATAM.
